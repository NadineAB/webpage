About Me

I am currently a third-year PhD student in the Department of Computer, Control, and Management Engineering at Sapienza University of Rome. My advisor is Prof. Marco Schaerf. Before that, I worked with my co-advisor Prof. Marco Fratarcangeli. During the third year of my PhD, I spent four months as a visiting researcher in Strasbourg working in the ICube Lab at University of Strasbourg. I am very passionate about animation, games, film and their creation. My goal is to gain experience and contribute my technical and programming skills in physics-based animation.

I am generally interested in most areas of Computer Graphics and Animation. My primary area of research involves the physically based simulation of deformable bodies. In particular, I focus on mesh deformation, character skinning and computational physics. I am also interested in fast collision detection and contact handling. The main applications are character animation, computer games and surgery simulation.
