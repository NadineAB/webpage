About Me

 

 

I am currently a third-year PhD student in the Department of Computer, Control, and Management Engineering at Sapienza University of Rome. My advisor is Prof. Marco Schaerf. Before that, I worked with my co-advisor Prof. Marco Fratarcangel. I am very passionate about animation, games, film and their creation. My goal is to gain experience and contribute my technical and programming skills in physics-based animation. My primary research interest focuses on physics-based animation of deformable bodies, where I am investigating skinning methods for skeleton-driven characters.

In particular, I focus on mesh deformation, character skinning and robust simulations for soft bodies. The main application is character animation in real-time. In addition, I am also interested in fast collision detection and contact handling.
