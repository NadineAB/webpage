About Me

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Computer Science Research Institute of Toulouse (IRIT) working with Prof. Dr. Loïc Barthe in the STORM Group on the topic of muscle deformations for virtual characters. My current position is funded by CIMI (Centre International de Mathématiques et Informatique de Toulouse) after a successful grant acquisition for 2 years. Prior, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen-Nürnberg , in the Institute for Multiscale Simulation (MSS) led by Prof. Dr. Thorsten Poschel , where I worked on coupling fluids and deformable bodies. I completed my Ph.D. studies in the ALCOR Lab at Sapienza University of Rome under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Marco Schaerf and Prof. Dr. Marco Fratarcangeli. I obtained my Ph.D. diploma in March 2016. My dissertation is entitled "Position-based Skin Deformations for Interactive Character Animation".

Research Interests

Computer Graphics and Animation: My primary area of research includes interactive character animation, real-time skinning, deformation, finite element methods, particle-based simulations, smoothed particle hydrodynamics, implicit skinning, collision detection, contact, and interactive physics.

Vision and Perception: I am also intersted in Computer vision and visual learning: SLAM, 3D reconstruction, feature detection, pattern recognition, structure from motion, and visual learning.
